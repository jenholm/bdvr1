# Limitations

## Proxy limitations

1. **Unresolved scalars**: The xGASS and SPARC proxies use global, unresolved measurements (inclination, linewidth, gas fraction, surface brightness). They do not resolve the proposed BDVR organization coordinate.

2. **Inclination-linewidth entanglement**: The xGASS coherence proxy explicitly includes both inclination and W50 linewidth. These are not independent — inclined galaxies have broader linewidths due to projection, and measurement uncertainty in either propagates to the proxy.

3. **Gas fraction degeneracy**: Gas fraction (1 - f_atm) correlates with both stellar mass and star formation activity. The proxy does not distinguish between a "organized" galaxy and a simply gas-poor one.

## Sample limitations

4. **SPARC selection**: SPARC is not volume-limited. Galaxies were selected for accessible rotation curves, which biases toward larger, brighter galaxies.

5. **xGASS H I bias**: The H I-detected xGASS subsample over-represents gas-rich galaxies. Gas-poor galaxies (which may be more "organized" in the BDVR sense) are underrepresented.

6. **Overlapping samples**: SPARC and xGASS have minimal overlap. Cross-survey comparisons assume consistent proxy behavior across independent samples.

## Statistical limitations

7. **Modest sample sizes**: After quality cuts (SPARC Q=1, xGASS H I-detected with good flags), the samples are ~87 and ~350 galaxies respectively. Quartile bins have ~20-90 galaxies.

8. **Multiple comparisons**: The ablation analysis tests 10+ proxy variants without formal multiple-testing correction.

9. **Causality**: Observed correlations between proxies and BTFR scatter do not establish a causal BDVR mechanism.

## Decisive test requirement

The scalar-proxy approach cannot distinguish between BDVR organization and mundane recoverability effects (inclination, projection, measurement precision). A decisive test requires resolved H I kinematics from interferometric observations (e.g., THINGS, VIVA, or new VLA/ASKAP/MeerKAT data) to compute kinematic asymmetry, lopsidedness, warp indices, and non-circular motion fractions.
