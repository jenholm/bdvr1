# M41 Falsifiable Predictions

1. Gas-rich, low-stellar-fraction systems with disordered H I velocity fields should fall below the mature BDVR velocity endpoint even when their baryonic mass would predict a higher saturated response.
2. Among gas-rich dwarfs, BDVR residuals should correlate more strongly with rotation-field coherence, H I asymmetry, depletion time, and SFE than with total gas fraction alone.
3. UGC07125-like cases should show weak reservoir activation only if independent H I data confirm unsettled geometry, poor endpoint reliability, inefficient star formation, or incomplete disk organization.
4. Mature activated candidates should show regular H I velocity fields, stable inclination, symmetric approaching/receding sides, normal SFE, coherent stellar disks, and low endpoint ambiguity.

These are data-acquisition predictions, not proof that BDVR is real.
